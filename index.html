Overview

This unit introduces key aspects of computer science including data storage and manipulation, and problem solving using a high-level programming language. Topics: the components of a modern computer system; fundamental concepts of data storage; function of operating systems; algorithms and problem solving; fundamental aspects of a programming language including data types, input/output, simple selection and iteration control structures, procedural and data abstraction, one-dimensional arrays and strings. Learning Outcomes

1. Develop practical application of skills needed to design, implement, and support network security. 2. Identify and describe network security principles. 3. Design and implement a secure network infrastructure. 4. Extend security and availability with cryptography and VPNs.

Assessments Expand All

The LMS quizzes will take place after each module and will examine the student’s understanding of the material covered in the module (Readings, Lectures and Lab exercises). Each quiz will typically consist of 10 to 20 multiple choice questions and the results will be released after the due date. Weight: 10 Due: Mid Individual / Group: Individual Unit learning outcomes: UNLO1, UNLO2, UNLO3, UNLO4
The in class exercises will typically take place at the beginning of each workshop and will measure the student’s understanding of material that was covered in the previous classes. The lecturer will go over the answer(s) after the students have submitted their exercise. Students must be present to take the exercises and have them count towards their overall unit mark. Weight: 10 Due: Continuous Individual / Group: Individual Unit learning outcomes: UNLO1, UNLO2, UNLO3, UNLO4
The final exam will be held during the University examination period. The exam will be a 3 hour comprehensive exam covering all of the topics covered in the unit. The exam will be conducting using LMS and may feature multiple choice, short answer, calculation and longer answer questions. Students must pass the final exam to pass the unit. Weight: 50 Due: Late Individual / Group: Individual Unit learning outcomes: UNLO1, UNLO2, UNLO3, UNLO4
The practical exam will be a 3 hour exam which will examine lab exercises, practical exercises and the case study. This exam will require you to troubleshoot and configure network devices under exam conditions. Students must pass the practical exam to pass the unit. Weight: 30 Due: Late Individual / Group: Individual Unit learning outcomes: UNLO1, UNLO2, UNLO3, UNLO4
